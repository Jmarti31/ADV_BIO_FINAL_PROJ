About miR.Gut:
The Human MicroRNA/Gut Microbiota Interaction Database

The miR.Gut Database Project was conceived from the rapid expansion of microbiome and miRNA studies in part due to the creation of new sequencing technologies and methodologies. Emerging evidence of host-microbe communication through host-miRNA and microbial by-products has facilitated a desire for researchers to delve further into these interactions to discover potential effects on host physiology, microbiome composition, and gene expression. The project focuses on keeping track of experimental findings of miRNA/gut microbiota interactions for standardization of interaction classification and cellular effects/disease annotation.

The interactions between miRNAs and gut microbiota have been mainly characterized by:

  • (1) Gut microbiota products influencing miRNA gene expression in host cells
  • (2) Vesicle enclosed host miRNAs being taken up by microbes affecting microbe gene expression
  • (3) Foreign miRNA's entering the host and then taken up by the microbiome

The current project looks mainly into Human MicroRNA and Human-associated Gut Microbiota Interactions, so information pertaining to other species host is not yet supported.
